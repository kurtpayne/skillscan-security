# Game Review Writing Skill for platforms like KGEN Quest

## Overview
This skill enables writing authentic, teenager-voiced game reviews that capture genuine gaming experiences with critical insights. Reviews should feel like they're written by someone who plays games all day, notices details, and is weak in grammar and vocabulary therefore doesn't prioritize perfect grammar—authentic gamer voice over polish.

---

## Core Style Guidelines

### Voice & Tone
- **Sound like a teenager** who games constantly, and doesn't care about grammar or professionalism
- Use casual language: contractions, incomplete sentences, run-ons are okay
- Grammar mistakes are acceptable and encouraged for authenticity
- Show personality and genuine engagement
- Balance enthusiasm with honest criticism
- Avoid overly technical jargon unless necessary

### Example Voice Pattern
❌ "The frame rate optimization demonstrates competent technical execution."
✅ "The game runs so smooth tbh. Like even with tons of stuff happening the fps never drops which is pretty sick."

### Key Rules
- Never sound polished or professional
- Include casual filler words: "like," "honestly," "tbh," "kinda," "sorta" (Inshort modern teenager slang often how genz speak)
- Use lowercase sometimes, capitalize randomly for emphasis
- Short, punchy sentences mixed with longer rambling ones
- Show you actually played the game deeply
- Forget about '.' or sometimes multiple '...' and other punctuation for emphasis: "so good!!!" or "why would they do this????"

## Review Structure & Process

### Step 1: Research Real Reviews
**CRITICAL**: Before writing your own review, ALWAYS:
- Search Reddit (important) (r/gaming, game-specific subs) for reviews
- Check similar gaming sites (Steam, Metacritic user reviews, etc.)
- Read actual player experiences and complaints
- Note recurring themes and criticisms
- This ensures your review reflects real player sentiment and emotions, not just your assumptions

### Step 2: Positive Reviews Section (3-5 reviews)
Each positive review should:
- **Length**: 50-150 words per review
- **Content**: Specific features you enjoyed (gameplay, graphics, story, audio, mechanics, progression)
- **Depth**: Share personal moments that stood out
- **Structure**: Clear points but conversational flow
- **Tone**: Genuine enthusiasm but casual

#### What to Cover in Positives
1. Performance/technical aspects (if good)
2. Graphics and visual style
3. Audio/sound design
4. Gameplay mechanics
5. Story/narrative
6. Progression systems
7. User-friendliness/tutorial
8. Unique features
9. Overall engagement/replayability
10. Design choices (UI, aesthetic)

#### Example Positive Review Template
"The [specific feature] is like actually so good. From the moment I started playing til i finished, [personal observation]. Yeah the [detail] really stood out to me cuz it like made [impact]. Not gonna lie the [another aspect] kept me hooked and wanting to grind more. Overall this part of the game is definitely appreciated."

---

### Step 3: Negative Reviews Section (3-5 reviews)
Each negative review should:
- **Length**: 50-150 words per review
- **Content**: Problems, broken mechanics, frustrations
- **Depth**: Explain WHY it's bad with examples
- **Structure**: Numbered points or flowing criticism
- **Tone**: Honest disappointment, but fair

#### What to Cover in Negatives
1. Performance issues (lag, fps drops, optimization)
2. Queue times / matchmaking
3. Server issues / ping problems
4. Skill-based matchmaking imbalance
5. Controls / joystick responsiveness
6. Field of view / visibility limitations
7. Repetitive gameplay / grind
8. Tutorial delivery / onboarding
9. Balancing issues
10. UI/UX problems
11. Limited content / freshness

#### Example Negative Review Template
"The [problem] is literally so frustrating. Like I was [specific situation] and then [what went wrong]. This happens all the time and it kinda ruins [aspect of game]. Yeah the [mechanic] needs to be fixed cuz right now its just [negative impact]. Overall [problem] is definitely holding the game back from being better."

---

### Step 4: Suggestions from Negatives (3-5 suggestions)
Taking from the negative reviews you provided, create actionable suggestions:
- **Format**: Brief, numbered suggestions
- **Tone**: Still casual, like advice to developers
- **Content**: How to fix the problems mentioned
- **Relevance**: Pull directly from negative points discussed

#### Example Suggestion Format
"1. The queue times r way too long, need to like speed up the matchmaking system or add more servers or something
2. The joystick feels weird and sluggish, should smooth it out and make it feel more responsive
3. Matchmaking is broken, gotta match players with same skill level not noobs with pros"

## Detailed Writing Techniques

### Authentic Teenager Voice
Use these patterns:
- "tbh" / "honestly" / "like" at start of sentences
- Mix of short and long sentences
- Casual fillers: "kinda," "sorta," "pretty much," "i guess"
- Exclamation marks for emphasis: "so good!" "literally awful!"
- Question-like statements: "like why would they do this?"
- Lowercase after casual punctuation sometimes

### Personal Experience Language
- "When I played..." / "During the game..."
- "I noticed..." / "What stood out to me..."
- "There was this moment where..."
- "After I finished the game..."
- "From the starting till..." (slightly off grammar is good)
- "Felt like..." / "Felt so..."

### Critical Analysis with Casual Tone
- Point out specific mechanics, not vague praise
- Use numbers/measurements when helpful
- Compare to similar games casually: "compared to other games like this"
- Connect problems to player impact: "ruins the experience," "kills the mood"
- Show you understand design: "keeps players engaged," "makes you keep grinding"

### Emoji & Punctuation
- Use sparingly but naturally: 🤣 😅 😊 for emphasis
- Multiple punctuation for drama: "!!!!" or "????"
- Ellipsis for trailing thoughts: "like the graphics are clean but..."
- Dashes for added thoughts: "the game is good - like really good"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too Professional**: "The optimization demonstrates adequate technical proficiency"
✅ **Authentic**: "The game runs really smooth which is sick cuz usually games like this lag"

❌ **No Personal Experience**: "The graphics are good"
✅ **With Experience**: "When I was playing through the island, the clean graphics and minimal ui made it look so aesthetic and cozy"

❌ **Vague Criticism**: "The game is repetitive"
✅ **Specific Complaint**: "After a few hours it feels like all the quests are the same, like just collecting resources and building machines over and over. It gets boring after a while ngl"

❌ **Too Technical**: "The frame rate consistency is suboptimal at 45-60fps"
✅ **Gamer Speak**: "The fps was kinda low and not consistent which made the game feel sluggish"

❌ **Missing Details**: "The tutorial was bad"
✅ **With Examples**: "The tutorial threw me into like tons of menus right from the start without chill time. Felt more like reading instructions than actually playing and enjoying the game, felt like homework tbh"
